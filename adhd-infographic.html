The
ADHD
Brain

ADHD isn't a lack of focus — it's an interest-based nervous system that struggles with regulation, not ability. These are the six core challenges.

1
Executive
Dysfunction

The brain's CEO is on break. Planning, prioritizing, starting tasks, and switching between them all require executive function — the control system that's under-resourced in ADHD.

Affects 89% of ADHD adults
2
Dopamine
Deficit

It's neurochemistry, not willpower. The ADHD brain underproduces dopamine, making "boring but important" tasks feel painful to start. The reward system demands novelty or urgency.

Transporters ~70% more active
3
Time
Blindness

Two times exist: "now" and "not now." Deadlines feel abstract until they're emergencies. Hours vanish during interesting tasks. Minutes crawl during boring ones.

3x more likely to miss deadlines
4
Emotional
Dysregulation

The overlooked dimension. ADHD emotions arrive faster, hit harder, and take longer to fade. Rejection sensitivity and frustration intolerance aren't character flaws — they're wiring.

~70% report regulation issues
5
Working
Memory Gaps

Your mental whiteboard holds 2 items instead of 7. Instructions evaporate mid-sentence. You walk into rooms forgetting why. Ideas vanish before you write them down.

Capacity ~30% lower
6
Hyperfocus
Trap

The paradox superpower. When something clicks, the brain locks in for hours — skipping meals, ignoring everything. But it picks you, not the other way around. The crash is the trap.

Episodes last 4-8 hrs
